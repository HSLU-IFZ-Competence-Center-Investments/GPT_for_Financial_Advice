Fuelling the transition to net-zero with Renewable Natural Gas

Biomethane, also known as renewable natural gas (RNG), is a carbon-neutral substitute for conventional fossil-based natural gas. Our small-scale, distributed plants process agri-food waste to produce biomethane in both compressed gas (Bio-CNG) and liquid (Bio-LNG) forms for both vehicular use and industrial use, as well as direct grid injection.

Bio-CNG & Bio-LNG for transport

At CycleØ, we produce and deliver both Bio-CNG and Bio-LNG for vehicular applications.

Green fuel for cleaner and better transport

The transport industry is responsible for nearly one-third of Europe’s total greenhouse gas emissions. Biofuels like Bio-LNG and Bio-CNG are the most readily available solution to decarbonise this critical sector.

When compared to diesel, HGV and transport fleets using renewable natural gas benefit from:

  • A reduced carbon footprint of up to 90%
  • Nearly 3x greater action radius
  • Up to 200% better fuel economy
  • Up to 85% less particulate emissions
  • As much as 50% less engine noise

From gas to liquid to market – seamlessly

We produce Bio-LNG using small-scale, modular liquefaction plants. Biomethane gas produced from our upgrading facilities is injected into a connected liquefaction unit, where it’s cooled to approximately -150 degrees Celsius using nitrogen until it reaches a liquid state. Bio-LNG is stored in tanks and can be easily transported by specialized tanker trucks to any location.

Solutions for RNG grid injection

The upgrading technology in our plants produces biomethane that’s 99% pure and suitable for direct injection into any grid that carries conventional natural gas. That means our facilities can be directly connected to any existing natural gas grid.

Virtual pipelines for off-grid applications

Our modular, skid-mounted plants are ideally suited for remote locations, where feedstock is easily accessible but connecting to a gas grid isn’t always possible. We partner with grid operators and provide virtual pipeline infrastructure and logistics to ensure the RNG we produce contributes to decarbonising the grid.
